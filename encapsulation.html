The Philosophy: Protective Wrapping & Encapsulation

At its core, Encapsulation is the sovereign principle of Information Hiding. It is the architectural boundary that separates the "What" from the "How." In the Weaver's Realm, we do not simply write code; we build fortresses where the internal state is shielded from the chaotic outside world.

By bundling data (attributes) and the behaviors that manipulate them (methods) into a single logical unit, we achieve Modular Autonomy. This ensures that an object‚Äôs internal representation is hidden from the outside, allowing only authorized interactions through a controlled interface.

Encapsulation is the "Veil" that protects the inner logic of your creation. In Python, we treat developers as "consenting adults," using conventions to signal where one should not tread.
